Initial Thoughts

The first thing that became apperent was the tickets model was pretty complicated. In fact after speaking to every department within the company, no body could correctly tell me the difference with out making at least one error, Except the sales department who dealt with people on the phone everyday going through the ticket options.

The sheer amount of different tickets was probly the biggest problem, but this was not somthing I could just go in and change, it would need management to buy in.

I set out to analise all of the ticket options.

Original Ticket Page

Tito Handling Payments

We use a third party company called Tito to handle the payments for our tickets. A widget from tito is inserted into the pricing page so any changes will have to take this into account, Also anything after the user clicks continue is set by Tito so cant really be changed.


Payment Checkout

What are all of these tickets?

There are potentially six different ticket types for a single event, I wanted to give an easier breakdown of these tickets to see what they were made up. It turns out they are just different combinations of three different components.

Components of a ticket

Wait theres even more tickets?

Tito has a feature that let the sales team offer add ons/ up sells onto each purchase, which on the face of it seems like a good idea a bit like having items by the check out, how ever these werent able to be tracked by Tito to see how effective they were.

Also the add on would make ticket options that werent originally displayed on the price page, Eg a one day pass could have an ad-on that made it effectivley a one day gold pass or one day silver pass

Upgrade Option

Analysis Polarisis

Whilst I waited for management to consider my ideas I went ahead and continued to see what a page with every ticket type would look like. This would also give them an idea of what a new ticket page would look like with six ticket types.
When there are too many options to choose from, it becomes difficult to process exactly what it is you want.

Ticket Table Comparison

Problem
Having 6 ticket types for a single event is overkill and leads to bottlenecks at the pricing page. The table does help to compare however it is still too complicated and trying to make it clear on mobile will be challenging

Reducing the amount of ticket options

I tried to make a proposal to reduce the amount of ticket types, So this could potentially make a huge positive difference and increase sales or have a huge negative impact and reduce the amount of sales, So a cautious approach was taken

On Demand

On Demand is one of our other sites, where we sell subscriptions to get access to our video library of all our event recordings and presentaions slides.
This is ticket is purely for people who cant make the event and want to buy the recorded presentations and slides of the event. Sales of these tickets were extremley rare

On Demand Card

Problem
Make this ticket secondary to the other tickets, but still available from the pricing page

Replacing with a link

Solution
Removing the OnDemand Card removes one of the pricing cards but still lets a user know that there is an option if they cant physically make the event

1 Day Pass

So the one day pass is identical to the silver pass except for it only gives access to one day of the event, after this pass is selected Tito allowed the sales team to put add ons to that pass that are equal to a one day pass for every ticket type

New Ticket Components

Problem
The one day pass takes up a ticket card and space on the table but is the same as a one day silver pass

Solution
Adding the 1 day pass as a secondary option to a other tickets means it doesnt need a seperate card, or to go onto the table

New Ticket Model

After a meeting with the sales dept and the company head we went forward with removing some of the less profitable and less popular tickets, It wasnt quite my proposal of totally removing the single event On Demand Pass, but was happy enough at getting the amount of tickets removed
