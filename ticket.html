Identifying problems

After the first round of user testing it became apparent there are two main issues that need improving on.

Problem 1: too may ticket types

The amount of different ticket options makes it difficult to choose between and leads to users taking too much time to decide which ticket to buy.

Problem 2: not easy to compare

The second problem is that its not easy to compare the ticket types there are a few reasons for this.

Firstly the way the ticket table is laid out, A vertical layout makes it harder to compare as each ticket because all its components are on a new line so the eye cant just scan across to the next ticket, instead it has to scan up then down again. As we read horizontally we become used to scanning text this way, scanning up and down doesn’t come as naturally.

Also it seems like the amount of descriptive text on each ticket is made to look as much as possible I think this was intentional as a sales technique to look like you get alot for your ticket.

Vertical payment table

Changing to a horizontal layout

By changing the layout from vertical columns to horizontal columns it makes it easier to compare

What are all of these tickets?

There are potentially six different ticket types for a single event, I wanted to give an easier breakdown of these tickets to see what they were made up. It turns out they are just different combinations of three different components.

Components of a ticket

If you get access to all of OnDemand that will include the single event automatically, So the single event access is just like a restricted access to OnDemand

Wait there are even more tickets?

Tito has a feature that let the sales team offer add ons/ up sells onto each purchase, which on the face of it seems like a good idea a bit like having items by the check out, however these weren't able to be tracked by Tito to see how effective they were.

Also the add on would make ticket options that weren't originally displayed on the price page, Eg a one day pass could have an ad-on that made it effectively a one day gold pass or one day silver pass.
