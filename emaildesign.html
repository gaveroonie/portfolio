Email Design is complicated

When I worked as part of a web development team every so often marketing would swing by and ask one of the developers to help fix or even redesign a marketing email template, this was usually met with developers becoming very busy very suddenly,.
I never understood why they hated it soo much, and as it was a job that no developer really wanted I thought it would be a good place to start to learn to code.

What I found out very quickly

Email design uses the same (CSS & html) code as a website but anything added since say the year 2000 hasnt quite made it to email, or at least not to all mail clients. Tables is the main technique used to layout the elements in an email. Tables havent been used in web design since the 2000’s.

"The phrase code like its 90’s is often thrown around when talking about email development."

The difference between one mail client and another is vast, Apple mail clients are great and i think give you the most freedom, Microsoft Outlook is a thorn in any email developers side. I think they have improved alot but since emails need to work on older versions it is still a problem today.

Its like the difference between producing somthing that works on a black and white TV a color TV & a HD state of the Art TV and trying to get somthing that looks great on all of them is very difficult.

Not all cards are equal

I wanted to explore my options on what I could do with the ticket cards, So after speaking to the sales team I found that the lowest sales were OnDemand and 1 day passes and they were both in single figures for the year, The top revenue was made off of three main tickets Silver, Gold and Diamond

On Demand

On Demand is one of our other sites, where we sell subscriptions to get access to our video library of all our event recordings and presentation slides.
This is ticket is purely for people who can't make the event and want to buy the recorded presentations and slides of the event. Sales of these tickets were extremely low.

On Demand Card

Problem

Replacing with a link

Solution
Removing the On Demand Card removes one of the pricing cards but still lets a user know that there is an option if they can't physically make the event.

1 Day Pass

So the one day pass is identical to the silver pass except for it only gives access to one day of the event, after this pass is selected Tito allowed the sales team to put add ons to that pass that are equal to a one day pass for every ticket type.

New Ticket Components

Problem
The one day pass takes up a ticket card and space on the table but is the same as a one day silver pass.

Solution
Adding the one day pass as a secondary option to a the other tickets means it doesn't need a separate card, or to go onto the table.
